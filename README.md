## Project Title

30-Day Hospital Readmission Analysis

---

## Overview

This project analyzes hospital readmission patterns to identify factors contributing to patients being readmitted within 30 days of discharge. The goal is to uncover trends and insights that can help healthcare providers reduce readmission rates, improve patient outcomes, and avoid financial penalties.

---

## Business Problem

Hospitals face significant challenges with high 30-day readmission rates, which lead to increased costs, regulatory penalties, and reduced quality of care. The key challenge is identifying which patients are most at risk of readmission and understanding the underlying factors driving these outcomes so that targeted interventions can be applied.

---

## Dataset

The dataset includes patient-level hospital records containing demographic information, admission details, medical conditions, length of stay, discharge information, and readmission indicators. This data is used to analyze patterns and identify risk factors associated with readmissions.

---

## Tools Used

SQL | Power BI | Data Modeling | Data Analysis | DAX

---

## Key Analysis

* Analyzed readmission rates across patient demographics such as age groups and gender
* Evaluated the impact of length of stay and discharge type on readmission likelihood
* Identified patterns in chronic conditions and comorbidities linked to higher readmission rates
* Examined trends in readmissions over time to detect consistency or seasonal variation
* Segmented patients based on risk indicators to highlight high-risk groups

---

## Results and Insights

* Patients with multiple chronic conditions showed significantly higher readmission rates
* Shorter initial hospital stays were associated with increased likelihood of readmission
* Certain discharge types (e.g., home without follow-up care) correlated with higher readmissions
* Older patient populations demonstrated a higher risk of returning within 30 days
* A small segment of high-risk patients accounted for a large portion of total readmissions
